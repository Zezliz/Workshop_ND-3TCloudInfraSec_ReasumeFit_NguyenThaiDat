Below are my personal opinions and feedback after completing the **First Cloud Journey (FCAJ)** practical training in coordination with **AWS Study Group**, aimed at helping the organizing team improve the program quality for future cohorts:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Learning Environment & Format**  
The self-paced online learning format combined with weekly lab deadlines is highly flexible. This allowed me to proactively manage my learning schedule at home alongside my academic classes at **HUTECH University**. The guide website is user-friendly, although some lab guides still display older versions of the AWS Console, which sometimes made it difficult for a beginner like me to locate options that have been redesigned in the newer AWS interface.

**2. Support from Mentors & Administrators**  
The administrative team and mentors are very enthusiastic about answering questions in the community support channels. What I appreciated most is that they do not simply hand out answers; instead, they guide you on how to check for errors (such as reviewing Security Groups, verifying Route Tables, or inspecting IAM Policies) so that I can think critically and identify the root cause myself, leading to deeper retention.

**3. Alignment with Academic Major**  
The program is extremely practical and directly complements my **Computer Networks** major. At HUTECH, I mainly studied networking theory and hardware device configurations. The FCAJ program opened up a realistic view of cloud infrastructure (Cloud Infrastructure), such as partitioning VPC networks, managing routing tables, and handling security in a real-world AWS environment.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
In addition to technical knowledge of cloud services, I developed a habit of reading official technical documentation (AWS Documentation) and gained skills in writing reports with Markdown, as well as managing lab code using Git/GitHub. These are all essential, highly valuable supplementary skills for my future career.

**5. Community Culture & Sharing Spirit**  
The FCAJ community has an excellent knowledge-sharing culture. Requiring blog posts to share technical knowledge (such as the 3 blogs I posted on the group) is a great way to consolidate what has been learned. The offline events, like the **FCAJ Community Day** at the AWS Bitexco office, gave me the opportunity to connect and listen to career experiences from experienced seniors.

**6. Program Benefits**  
Even though this is a non-profit, community-driven self-learning program, the knowledge gained and the structured roadmap are extremely valuable and far exceeded the expectations of a student starting out in Cloud.

---

### Additional Questions & Suggestions

*   **What did you find most satisfying during the training?**  
    Being able to configure complex network architectures hands-on and having the opportunity to attend offline community events, connecting directly with leading cloud experts and practical engineers.
*   **What does the program need to improve for future cohorts?**  
    The organizing team should add a dedicated section or a compilation post of common errors and troubleshooting tips (e.g., Access Denied errors from IAM, timeouts from Security Groups, or routing table issues) for each weekly lab. These issues often cause beginners to spend days searching for solutions.
*   **If recommending to friends, would you advise them to participate?**  
    Absolutely. The program is designed in a highly detailed, step-by-step manner from easy to advanced levels, and is backed by an active, supportive community, which is perfect for students looking to learn Cloud.
*   **Suggestion to improve the learning experience:**  
    Update the screenshots in the lab guides to reflect the latest AWS Console interface to help future cohorts avoid confusion during their hands-on practice.
